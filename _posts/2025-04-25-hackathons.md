# My Hackathon Journey: Building Under Pressure

Hackathons are something I always look forward to. It's tiring for sure ‚Äî sometimes 24 hours straight, no proper sleep, just coding and surviving on kopi and snacks. But the experience? **Very worth it**.

Every hackathon pushes me to think fast, work with people from different backgrounds, and build something from scratch under pressure. Win or lose, I always learn something new ‚Äî whether it's a new framework, a better way to collaborate, or just how to present an idea clearly.

## Notable Hackathon Experiences

### üìå RHB Hack 2023 - My First Hackathon

**When**: January 2023 (during high school completion)  
**Project**: **Zee** - An expense tracking feature with Tinder-style swipe interface

> "Spend mindfully, even when you're not thinking clearly."

**What we built:**
- Tinder-style swipe interface for expense categorization
- Weekly feedback system based on spending choices
- Goal: Build better money awareness habits

**Tech Stack**: JavaScript, Next.js, React, Tailwind CSS, Chakra UI

**Result**: Didn't make Top 10 (out of 100 teams), but gained invaluable experience  
**Mentor**: [Khairul Nazran](https://www.linkedin.com/in/knazran/), one of Malaysia's top hackathon veterans

### üìå Startathon 23/24 Garage@EEE - First Place! üèÜ

**Project**: Crop tracking system for farmers to secure funding from SMEs and VCs

**System Features:**

  - Soil moisture monitoring
  - Temperature tracking

- Complete JavaScript and React frontend

**Demo Videos:**

## What I've Learned

### Technical Skills
- **Rapid Prototyping**: Building MVPs in 24-48 hours
- **Technology Diversity**: Exposure to various tech stacks
- **Full-Stack Development**: Frontend, backend, and mobile development
- **API Integration**: Working with external services under time pressure

### Soft Skills
- **Team Collaboration**: Working with diverse teams under pressure
- **Pitch Presentation**: Communicating ideas clearly and persuasively
- **Problem Solving**: Creative solutions with limited resources
- **Time Management**: Prioritizing features for maximum impact

### Industry Exposure
- **Blockchain/Web3**: Understanding decentralized applications
- **FinTech**: Payment systems and financial applications

## The Hackathon Mindset

Hackathons teach you to:
1. **Think Fast**: Rapid ideation and decision making
2. **Build Fast**: MVP development in hours, not weeks
3. **Fail Fast**: Quick iteration and learning from mistakes
4. **Present Fast**: Clear communication under pressure

## Why I Keep Going Back

Despite the exhaustion, the caffeine crashes, and the occasional disappointment, hackathons remain some of my most rewarding experiences. They're where:

- **Innovation Happens**: Pressure creates diamonds
- **Networks Form**: Meeting like-minded builders and creators  
- **Skills Develop**: Learning by doing, not just reading
- **Ideas Come to Life**: Seeing concepts become reality in hours

## Looking Forward

Each hackathon has shaped my approach to technology and problem-solving. Whether it's winning first place or learning from a loss, every experience adds to my toolkit as an engineer and innovator.
